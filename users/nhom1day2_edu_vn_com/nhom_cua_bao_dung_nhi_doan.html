02
Tourist Attractions

Highlights

Fascinating Karst Landscape
A system of thousands of limestone islets with unique shapes: Fighting Cock Islet, Incense Burner Islet, Toad Islet, Human Head Islet — each with its own story.
Magnificent Cave System
Sung Sot Cave, Thien Cung Grotto, Dau Go Cave — caves with tens-of-thousands-of-years-old stalactites and stalagmites, shimmering and mystical.
Geological Values
A geological formation history spanning approximately 500 million years and a sublime karst evolution process of over 20 million years — a living geological book.
Biodiversity
17 endemic plant species such as Ha Long Cycad and Ha Long Fan Palm, along with approximately 60 endemic animal species found nowhere else in the world.
Archaeological Sites
The cradle of prehistoric humans with the Soi Nhu, Cai Beo, and Ha Long cultures. The ancient Van Don commercial port — a trade hub since the 12th century. XII.
Tourism Experiences
5-star cruises, kayaking, and swimming at Ti Top and Ba Trai Dao islands. Visiting the ancient Cua Van and Vung Vieng fishing villages — life on the water.

04
Challenges

Current Threats

1
Waste & Wastewater Pollution
Over 200 tons of waste per year from tourism and fishing. Most domestic wastewater from coastal urban areas is discharged directly into the bay untreated.
2
Industrial Activities
Coal mining, coal silt handling, and the transportation of clinker and petroleum, along with coastal cement and thermal power plants, pose a risk of dust and oil pollution.
3
Illegal Economic Activities
Unplanned aquaculture and the use of styrofoam floats cause pollution and disrupt the landscape when these floats are dispersed across the bay.
4
Tourism Overload Pressures
The rapid growth in visitor numbers is overloading natural resources. Additionally, a segment of tourists and residents still lacks environmental awareness.
5
Climate Change
Storms and floods (such as Typhoon No. 3 in 2024) cause damage to ecosystems and infrastructure. Sea-level rise poses a direct threat to the island and cave systems.
⚠
Public Awareness
The lack of education and awareness regarding heritage values among a segment of local residents and domestic tourists remains a persistent problem.
05
Heritage Preservation

Plans for Future Conservation

Planning
Conservation Master Plan 2021–2030, vision 2050
Create plans for preservation, restoration, and recovery of scenic sites; adjust buffer zone boundaries for more effective management.
Environment
"Ha Long Bay Free from Plastic Waste" Program (2019)
Strictly ban single-use plastic products on the bay. Regularly organize campaigns to collect floating waste.
Waste Control
Wastewater treatment requirements for cruise ships
Mandatory installation of oil-water separators; replace styrofoam floats with sustainable buoyant materials in aquaculture.
Community Relocation
Relocating fishing villages to inland resettlement (since 2014)
Relocate households from fishing villages to protect the landscape and water environment while improving fishermen's living conditions.
Green Tourism
"Blue Sail" Eco-label for cruise ships
Ban high-speed motorboats in the core zone to protect biodiversity. Apply ecological criteria to all tourist vessels.
International Cooperation
Collaboration with UNESCO, IUCN, and JICA (Japan)
Receive technical, financial support and heritage management experience from leading international organizations.
06
International Recognition

Titles & Awards

1994
UNESCO World Heritage Site
First recognized by UNESCO for its outstanding aesthetic values and natural landscape.
2000
UNESCO Heritage Expansion
Re-recognized under additional criteria for its globally exceptional geological and geomorphological values.
2011
New7Wonders of Nature
Declared as one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature by the New7Wonders Foundation.
2023
Third UNESCO Recognition
Acknowledged for its biodiversity and ecosystem values, expanding the heritage boundaries to include the Cat Ba Archipelago.
